# Language!Live (L!L) Quick Reference Guide for ESS's

A quick reference for Educational Support Specialists using Language!Live components in the pacing guide application.

---

## 📋 Quick Start Checklist

### Beginning of Year Setup

1. **Week 1**: Place **L!L Startup** (3 days)
   - Introduces students to program, avatar creation, classroom procedures

2. **Day 4**: Place **L!L: Roster Students** (1 day)
   - Setup student accounts in online platform

3. **Day 5-6**: Place **BOY Benchmark: PAR2, TOSCRF-2, TWS-5** (1 day)
   - Beginning-of-year comprehensive benchmark
   - Tests for initial level placement

4. **Day 7**: Place **L!L: Benchmark Grouping** (1 day)
   - Analyze results and sort students into Level 1 or Level 2

### Weekly Instruction

5. **Weeks 2-37**: Place **Language! Live Units**
   - Use **22-day version** for standard 90-minute blocks
   - OR **10-day version** for accelerated/single-level classes
   - 12 units total × 3 weeks each = 36 weeks

6. **Mid-Year**: Place **MOY Benchmark** (1 day)
   - Around week 18-20
   - Progress check assessment

7. **End of Year**: Place **EOY Benchmark** (1 day)
   - Final comprehensive assessment

---

## 🎯 Understanding Level 1 vs Level 2

### Level 1: Foundational Skills
**Who**: Students significantly below grade level
**Focus**:
- Consonant and vowel sounds
- Basic decoding
- Phoneme/grapheme mapping
- Simple sentences and paragraphs

**Writing Projects**: Basic Paragraph, Problem and Solution, Simple Narratives

### Level 2: Advanced Skills
**Who**: Students approaching grade level
**Focus**:
- Syllable types and morphemes
- Greek and Latin roots
- Complex text comprehension
- Literary analysis

**Writing Projects**: Informational, Argument, Literary Analysis, Compare/Contrast

### How to Identify Which Level

After BOY Benchmark, use these guidelines:
- **Level 1**: Reading 2+ grade levels below
- **Level 2**: Reading 1 grade level below or approaching grade level

## 📚 Component Selection Guide

### When to Use Each Unit Component

| Component | Duration | Best For | Notes |
|-----------|----------|----------|-------|
| **L!L Unit (2 Days)** | 22 days | Mixed-level classrooms (90 min blocks) | Most common choice. Includes 2 data conferences |
| **L!L Unit (Single Day)** | 10 days | Single-level classrooms OR accelerated pacing | Both levels do TT & WT same day |
| **L!L Unit #, Lesson #** | 1 day | Makeup lessons, custom pacing | Flexible single lesson |

### Unit Structure Comparison

**22-Day Unit (2 Days per Lesson)**:
```
Day 1: Lesson 1 - Level 1: TT / Level 2: WT
Day 2: Lesson 1 - Level 1: WT / Level 2: TT
Day 3: Lesson 2 - Level 1: TT / Level 2: WT
Day 4: Lesson 2 - Level 1: WT / Level 2: TT
Day 5: Data Conference
... (continues for 10 lessons)
Day 22: Lesson 10 complete
```

**10-Day Unit (Single Day per Lesson)**:
```
Day 1: Lesson 1 - Both levels complete TT & WT
Day 2: Lesson 2 - Both levels complete TT & WT
... (continues for 10 lessons)
Day 10: Lesson 10 complete
```

## 💡 Common Questions

### Q: Do I need to place all assessments?
**A**:
- **Required**: BOY, MOY, EOY Benchmarks + Unit Post-Tests
- **Optional but recommended**: Unit Pre-Tests
- **Standalone**: PAR, TOSCRF-2, TWS-5 (only if testing separately from benchmarks)

### Q: What's the difference between the standalone tests and benchmarks?
**A**:
- **Benchmarks** (BOY/MOY/EOY) = all 3 tests combined (PAR2 + TOSCRF-2 + TWS-5)
- **Standalone** (PAR, TOSCRF-2, TWS-5) = use only if administering individually

Most schools use the comprehensive benchmarks, not standalone tests.

### Q: When should I use Data Conference?
**A**: Data Conferences are built into the 22-day units (2 per unit). Use the standalone component if you need additional data review time.

### Q: How do I know which writing project type to use?
**A**: The "L!L Writing Project" component includes all 8 types. When you place it on the calendar, you can rename it to specify which type (e.g., "L!L Writing Project: Argument Essay").

**Level 1 Types**: Basic Paragraph, Shared Scientific Research, Problem and Solution, Firsthand and Secondhand Accounts, Compare and Contrast Fairy Tales, Thematic Literature, Argument, Career Documents

**Level 2 Types**: Informational, Cause and Effect, Narrative, Argument, Compare and Contrast (Thematic Literature), Compare and Contrast (Fiction and Nonfiction), Literary Analysis, Career Documents

### Q: What if I have a 45-minute block instead of 90 minutes?
**A**: Use the **Split Class Model**:
- Monday/Wednesday/Friday: Text Training (TT)
- Tuesday/Thursday: Word Training (WT)

Or use the **Single Day units** if students can complete both TT and WT in 45 minutes.

### Q: Can I reorder components in a cell?
**A**: Yes! Use the up/down arrow buttons on each component. This is especially useful when you have multiple components on the same day.

### Q: What's "ReadingScape"?
**A**: Online wide reading component for independent practice and fluency building. Place 1-2 times per week or as scheduled.

## 🚀 Pro Tips

1. **Start Simple**: Use the 22-day unit for your first implementation
2. **Plan Writing Projects**: Schedule them at the end of units for best results
3. **Use Flex Days**: Place them before/after breaks for catch-up time
4. **Color Code**: Keep the default colors - they help identify component types
5. **Data Conferences**: These are your progress check-ins - don't skip them!
6. **State Testing**: Block out 1-2 weeks for state testing prep in spring
7. **Make-Up Components**: Keep a few in your library for schedule adjustments
